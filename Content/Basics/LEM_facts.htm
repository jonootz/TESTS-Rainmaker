Lease Expiration Management Tips

Here are some tips and best practices to keep in mind when using Lease Expiration Management.

  • The system considers four factors when determining the expiration recommendation for each month: Seasonality, Historical Move-ins and Move-outs, Historical Renewal Behavior, and Capacity of the Unit Category.
  • LEM overrides are made, saved, and re-optimized in a live environment and will be reflected in pricing recommendations immediately.
  • To understand the impact of an override, save the changes, then re-optimize and review the new lease pricing for the affected unit type.
  • Premiums entered for Lease Term Categories will have a greater impact than any overrides made on the Expiration Management Graph.
  • LEM is part of both the optimization process and the post-optimization process.
  • Expiration Management premiums, Rec Gen premiums, and Smoothing are factored in during post-optimization after the LEM calculations are completed.
  • Smoothing over Weeks is post processing functionality which tunes down steep price changes from one move-in week to another. The smoothing can be tuned down using parameters 60 to 70 to give preference to LEM-based pricing.
  • When seasonality has been "seeded", the fixed seasonality data will populate in the Expiration Management graph.
  • LEM overrides for a particular month are persistent and carry over from year to year. Overrides will remain in the system until manually deleted or further modified.
  • Overrides should be made in such a way that the total recommended expiration in the year are preserved. When expirations for certain months are overridden up, expirations for other months would need to be overridden down.
  • If the total recommended expirations for the year need to be changed, all months may need to be overridden up or down. An example is the Lease-up case where the available historical data would not result in robust recommended expirations.
  • Use Expiration Management premiums as a brute-force method to completely shut down a series of months (say winter months).
  • With LEM, the recommended prices can be restricted to a maximum percentage over the reference rent using parameter 178. The maximum percentage that the system can price below the reference rent is controlled by parameter 41.
  • LEM uses the community level historical data to determine expiration counts for a unit category if it has fewer than 32 units. This number of units is set by parameter 173, which is set to 32 by default.
