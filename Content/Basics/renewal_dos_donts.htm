Renewal Dos and Don'ts

When handling renewal offers, it is important to be confident in your pricing, offers, and conversations. Here are some dos and don'ts to help you when working with renewals.

Don'ts Dos

Blame , Corporate, or someone else for the renewal rate.

Discuss potentially difficult conversations with PRM or Regional Manager, then take ownership of the decision

Tell the resident that once the rates are set and the letter sent, the rate cannot be changed.

If the resident is concerned with the increase, discuss their concerns and offer to research any questions and follow-up. If the increase is justified, honestly discuss the basis for the offer.

Have a renewal conversation with a resident without preparing first.

Review any available information regarding past increases, service issues, etc. to prepare for an in-person meeting with the resident.

Leave an increase letter at the door and run when you know it will upset the resident.

Make personal contact prior to sending an increase letter. Set up an appointment to meet and discuss the resident's renewal options.
