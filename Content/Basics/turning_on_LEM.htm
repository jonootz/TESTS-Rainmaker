Turning on Lease Expiration Management

These are the steps you should take if you are not currently using Lease Expiration Management and you want to turn it on.

  1. Save the current New Lease Pricing Detail (under LRO>New Pricing) by exporting it to Excel and saving the file. This will be used to provide a comparison of pre-LEM and post-LEM pricing recommendations.
  2. Set parameter 172 to a value of 1 (under Admin>Parameters). This enables the LEM functionality.
  3. Re-optimize pricing to generate the expiration and seasonality numbers on the Expiration Management graph (under LRO>Expiration Management).

  4. Review the recommended expirations on the Expiration Management Graph:

    Check the total recommended expirations in the year with respect to the unit category's capacity.

    Check the recommended expiration numbers for each month in the year.

  5. Override the recommendations, if needed, based on your review.
  6. Remove any existing monthly premiums in the Monthly Premiums screen (under LRO>Expiration Management). Also, remove any Rec Gen lease term premiums that may be in place (under Admin>Rec Gen).
  7. Reset the Lease Term Category premiums (under Configuration>LTC). For example, if it is acceptable to allow LEM to favor Medium lease terms over Long lease terms, set the premium for the Medium lease term category to 0%. Reference rent overrides will need to be made to the Medium LTC to reflect the modified premiums (under LRO>Reference Rent).
  8. Re-optimize and review the new LEM generated pricing (under LRO>New Pricing). Reference the saved pre-LEM pricing to see what impact enabling LEM had on the new pricing.
  9. Make reference rent overrides (under LRO>Reference Rent) as needed to align the LEM-based prices with your original prices. If any reference rent changes are made, then re-optimize to view updated pricing.
  10. Smoothing over weeks may restrict pricing differentiation for the weeks falling in the beginning or end of a month. The impact of smoothing can be lessened by changing the smoothing parameters (under Admin>Parameters). Smoothing can be turned off if parameter 65 is set to 100% and parameters 60 to 64 and 66 to 70 are set to 0%. Please note that turning off smoothing may lead to higher jumps in pricing from one move-in week to the next.
  11. If needed, re-enter Monthly Premiums (under LRO>Expiration Management) and Rec Gen Lease Term Premiums (under Admin>Rec Gen) and re-optimize pricing. Additional premiums can be used to achieve the business goals of the community.
