Renewal Strategies

There are many different renewal strategies. Two common strategies are resident retention and maximum revenue.

Resident Retention Strategy

Some key components of a resident retention strategy include:

  • Conservative Increases
  • Minimize Turnover Costs and Vacancy Loss
  • Incentives for Early Committal to Renew
  • Rewards for Length of Tenancy

This is a good strategic approach when the economy/market has weakened, the cost of home ownership is comparable to renting in the area, or you're planning to refinance or sell the property.

You're more likely to negotiate with residents when employing this strategy.

Maximum Revenue Strategy

Some key components of a maximum revenue strategy include:

  • More Aggressive Increases
  • Focus on Rent/Revenue Growth
  • Budget for Higher Turnover

This strategy works well in markets where it is expensive to move, expensive to buy a home, or expensive to rent at another community. Although you may get more turnover with this strategy, this could be a good thing in the long run if new residents will pay considerably more than the existing residents.

You may do some negotiating with this strategy, but you will still be focused on getting an increase for the unit.
