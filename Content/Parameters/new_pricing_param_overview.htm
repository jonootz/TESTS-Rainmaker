New Lease Parameters

The new lease parameters help you communicate your business strategy regarding new pricing to the system. These parameters are adjusted as the strategy for the community changes. Changes often correspond with seasonality when demand is expected to increase or to slow down during particular time periods. These parameters allow you to be aggressive, moderate, or conservative in your approach to pricing for new leases.

You should periodically review these parameter settings to make sure they correctly represent your new lease pricing strategy. You should also make sure that the parameter settings complement each other and are not working against each other.

Following are the new lease parameters you'll be most likely to change.

View suggested values for the New Pricing parameters

Exposure

The Exposure Thresholds are reflected in the Pricing Trend Report as High Exposure and Low Exposure bands.

71 Low Exposure Threshold

72 High Exposure Threshold

The Exposure Thresholds should be evaluated before changing parameter 153, the Exposed Units Range parameter.

Brake Factors determine to what extent the system will respond to growing exposure.

74 Brake Factors High

75 Brake Factors Medium

If you have unit types with low unit counts, for statistical purposes, you may want the DRRC to use exposure at the unit category level instead of the unit type level. You can set the low unit count threshold to determine when exposure at the unit category level should be used.

84 Threshold for low UT count (DRRC)

Reference Rent

The Aggressive Factor is the % increase applied to the reference rent in times of low exposure and good leasing velocity.

73 Aggressive Factor

You can specify the maximum reference rent decrease allowed in a week (assuming the DRRC is run on a weekly basis).

152 Max Ref Rent Decrease

Reference rent can be adjusted by business rules involving weeks of zero recent leasing. Learn about parameter 110 Weeks of Zero RL

Leasing Velocity

You can set upper and lower boundaries for leasing velocity.

107 Low Leasing Velocity Threshold

108 High Leasing Velocity Threshold

Learn about parameters 88 and 104 which can also affect leasing velocity

Maximum Price Reduction

The Max Price Reduction parameter gives the optimizer some flexibility to go below the average competitor rents in an effort to re-stabilize the community or to discount units based on availability date when supply exceeds demand.

41 Max Price Reduction

If you define parameter 41 at the unit type level, parameter 243 is used to determine how parameter 41 will be propagated to the unit category level for optimization.

New Demand

Parameter 182 controls whether only new demand or both renewals and new demand are used in the new pricing calculation.

You should consult with a support or implementation specialist before changing this parameter.

182 New Demand
