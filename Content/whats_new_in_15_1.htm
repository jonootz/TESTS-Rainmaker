Supported browsers for the LRO Web UI are Microsoft Internet Explorer 9.x - 11.x, Google Chrome, and Firefox. If you use other browsers, your experience may not be optimal and some functionality may not work correctly.

Competitor Improvements

The following improvements have been made regarding competitors and competitor rents:

Comp Setup

  • It's now easier to identify and add competitors using a map which populates with your location, your competitors' locations, and locations of other communities.
  • You no longer have to enter a Comp ID when you set up a competitor. Also, the Comp ID was removed from any screens that used to display it.

Comp Rents

  • The Comp Rents screen has been updated with a new look and feel. It is now easier to view and enter your comp rent information.
  • You can now edit competitor unit types from the Comp Rents screen by clicking the Gear icon on the right side of the section for the unit type.
  • Pricing history is now displayed all of the time in an easy to read graph. You can see how the comp rents have been trending and mouse over the points on the graph to see the actual amounts and who entered them.
  • In addition to the unit type mapping, you can now see the position of the competitor unit type.

Competitor Unit Types

  • When you add a competitor, competitor unit types will automatically be created and mapped to a corresponding unit type. You will just need to review the competitor unit types to make sure the information is correct.

    You can enter the bed and bath information for the unit type to have it automatically entered for any new competitor unit types that get created.

  • Bed and bath information is now required for saving a competitor unit type.

The Competitor screens were removed from the Analyst tool. Entering and viewing competitor information should be done from the Web UI.

Automated Comps

Automated Comps makes it easy to enter your competitor rents each week. It automatically gets the rent information for the competitors you have set up to use Automated Comps. That means all you have to do is review the rent information instead of calling around or looking it up each week.

Automated Comps is a premium feature that is available for an additional fee. Contact your account representative for more information.

Learn more about Automated Comps

Days on Market Analysis

Days on Market Analysis would typically be used by Pricing Analysts.

To use Days on Market Analysis, parameter 276 must be set to 1 for the community.

To view Days on Market Analysis, go to LRO>Rev Mgmt>Days on Market Analysis.

Security

There is a new user right for Days on Market Analysis. It is called Field Days on Market Analysis and it allows users to access the Days on Market Analysis screen.

Upon upgrade to release 15.1, this right will be given to users who have the following rights:

  • Administrator
  • PricingAnalyst
  • LRO_Administrator
  • LRO_PricingAnalyst

Online Help

Online help is now available in the system. You can click the info button at the top right side of the screen to open help.

This replaces the PDF guides that used to be created for each release. Now this information is available at your fingertips when you are using the system. You can search the help content or use the navigation on the left side of the help screen to find the information you need.

You can print a help topic using the Print button.

If you have done a search, the search terms will be highlighted when viewing the topics that are found. You can press the Remove Highlights button to remove the highlighting.

New Availability & Pricing Report (BI Reporting)

The Availability and Pricing Report allows you to view all percentage change metrics for occupancy and rents in one place. On the Detail view, you can see the information broken out by community and then by unit type, if you want that level of detail. This report would typically be used by pricing managers on a weekly basis.

There are two views of the report, Overview and Detail, plus a Definitions page.

  • The Overview gives you a graphical representation of your occupancy and your average rent as of the report date, as well as last week, last month, last year, and the first of the year.
  • The Detail View displays your availability and pricing information and comparisons in a table format. You can show the comparisons as a percentage change or the actual measures.

Learn more about the Availability & Pricing Report

New Daily Pulse Report (BI Reporting)

The Daily Pulse report gives you a feel for where communities stand today in regards to recommended rent, exposure, occupancy, and average leases signed over the last 60 days. You can also look at the rent and exposure for the selected report date as compared with the prior day. This report is typically used by regional managers and owners.

There are two views of the report, Overview and Detail, plus a Definitions page.

  • The Overview contains a graph displaying a 30 day trend of recommended rents.
  • The Detail View gives you recommended rent comparisons at the community and unit type levels.

Learn more about the Daily Pulse Report

Rent Trend Report Improvements

The following enhancements were made to the Rent Trend Report.

  • The report was updated with a completely new design.
  • The report now has an Overview page which provides a graphical view of your rent trend information.
  • The Detail view has been reformatted so it is easier to read and can be printed.
  • The report now displays 6 quarters of information, instead of 5.
  • You can filter the report for a specific quarter or selection of quarters.
  • You can drill down on the Overview and the Detail view to easily see the rent trend information at different hierarchy levels.
  • A Definitions page was added to help you understand the information on the report.

You can take snapshots of this report now that it is "drillable".

Weekly Report Improvements

The following enhancements were made to the Weekly Report.

  • When you open the report, there is an optional prompt to control how you want your traffic information to be displayed on the report. If you don't select anything here, the traffic information will be displayed for the past 7 days. You can select to display your traffic information for the past 7, past 14, or past 30 days.
  • Occupancy, Exposure, and Renewal % are now displayed out to one decimal place.
  • You can filter the Visits and Leases information by number of days. It defaults to whatever was selected from the Prompts screen when you opened the report. Your filter options are past 7 days, past 14 days, past 30 days, and default. This filter is persistent across tabs that use this filter.
  • On the KPI Focus Page and the Asking & Effective Rents page, there is a new column called Asking Rent vs All Leases. This compares your current asking rent with the de-amenitized average effective rent for all leases. This allows you to see how much more/less you would be getting in rent if you replaced all of your current leases with new leases.

  • On the KPI Focus Page and the Asking & Effective Rents page, there is a new filter that allows you to display the rent differences as a percentage or as a dollar amount. This affects the following columns:

    • Asking Rent vs All Leases
    • Move Ins Higher than Expiring
    • Renewals Higher than Expiring
  • On the Asking & Effective Rents page, the columns under Avg Eff Rent Comparison are now in a more logical order.
