Revenue Per Unit (RPU) Report (BI Reporting)

The Revenue Per Unit (RPU) report can be accessed from the Reporting tab in the Web UI.

This report gives you an indication of how a community's occupancy and rents are impacting revenue. RPU is calculated by multiplying the number of occupied units by the average rent of the occupied units and then dividing by the total number of units. The information used in these calculations comes from the Summary Analysis report.

What questions can the RPU report answer?

Viewing the Report

The report will open when you click the Revenue Per Unit link on the Reporting tab.

There are two ways to view this report, with RPU as a bar or with RPU as a line.

RPU Shown as a Bar

RPU Shown as a Line

Viewing RPU as a bar allows you to quickly compare the RPU with the average rent for your occupied units since average rent is represented by a bar. Viewing RPU as a line allows you to quickly see how your RPU is trending with your occupancy which is represented by a line.

Understanding the Information on the Report

The filter selections you make will determine the units that are used in the calculations on the report. For example, you may filter the report to only show units at one community or you could filter the report to only show units of a particular unit type.

You're able to see information for the past 17 months so you can look for any trends and compare this year to last year.

RPU: This is the revenue per unit for the month represented. The calculation is Occupancy*Avg Rent Occupied Units. The Y-axis on the left side of the graph is used to chart the average revenue values.

RPU can be shown as a bar or as a line on the graph.

Avg Rent Occupied Units: This is the average rent for all units that were occupied in the month represented. The Y-axis on the left side of the graph is used to chart the average rent values.

Occupancy: This is the percentage of units that were occupied in the month represented. The Y-axis on the right side of the graph is used to chart the occupancy percentage.
