Renewals Report (BI Reporting)

The Renewals Report can be accessed from the Reporting tab in the Web UI.

This report provides useful information about your renewals. Pricing Analysts can use this report to make decisions on renewal strategy and even whether or not to re-negotiate offers if a large number of notices have come in. Regional Managers and Site Teams can use this report to gauge progress for upcoming expirations and follow up with residents who are undecided or have given notice.

What questions can the Renewals Report answer?

There are two views of the report, Overview and Detail, plus a Definitions page. You can switch between them using the tabs at the bottom of the screen.

All lease counts on this report are based on lease termination date. The lease termination date is the move out date or the lease expiration date, whichever is first.

Overview

After you select your hierarchy on the Prompts dialog and click OK, the report will open in the Overview. The Overview contains two graphs that help you review your expiring leases and renewals.

The Overview graphs show you:

  • how many leases are expiring in the month and the renewal status for those leases
  • the difference between the move in rent and the current rent for the leases that are expiring in the month

By default, the information shown on both graphs is for the current month, but you can use the Input Controls to view it for the next month, two months out, or three months out, as well.

Up-Down information is not available three months out.

Monthly Counts

This graph shows you a breakdown of the status of the leases that are expiring in the displayed month.

This gives you insight into your current month expirations. You can see how many of the expiring residents have renewed, given notice, or are undecided.

Variance Graph

This graph shows you the difference between the rent for new move ins vs the rent for the expiring leases. This is also known as Up - Down since it shows you if the rents are up or down. If it’s green, then the new move ins are higher than the expiring leases and if it’s red, then the new move ins are lower than the expiring leases. This information is useful for making decisions regarding renewal parameters and how aggressive or conservative to be.

If the new move ins are considerably higher, there is more room to grow renewal rents. If the rents are closer together or the new move-ins are less than the expiring leases, then it may make sense to incorporate a renewal strategy around a minimum increase.

This graph is not shown if you view the information for three months out.

Tip

You can drill down on the graphs by clicking on the hierarchy level on either graph. This will adjust both graphs to drill down to the next level in the hierarchy. To drill back up, you can click the Level on either graph.

Detail View

On the Detail view, you'll see a break down of information about your renewals in a table format for the current month plus the next three months. You can also see a summary of key information about your renewals over the last 60 days.

Up-Down information is not available three months out.

You can click on an attribute value in the Level column to drill down through the levels of the selected hierarchy.

You can select to view the information for all communities, a particular community, or a selection of communities.

You can control the level of detail you see on the report by viewing it at the hierarchy level, the hierarchy and community level, or at the hierarchy, community, and unit type level.

By default, the amounts on the report are shown with amenities, but you can choose to show them without amenities, if you want to.

Tip

When you choose to view the Detail View at the Level to Display of Hierarchy & Community or Hierarchy, Community & Unit Type, you can click on any of the renewal numbers to see which leases fall under each status.

The information will open in a new report.
