Rent Visualizer: LTC to LT Stage

The first stage of propagation is where lease expiration management is predominantly used. It factors in the actual expirations, remaining expirations, and the LRO recommended expirations to help determine which lease term should be favored and which lease terms should be discouraged. The values generated are at the unit category, individual lease term level.

Inputs

Input

Definition

Optimized Base Rent

Output from the optimization stage at the unit category, lease term category level for each pricing week in the optimization horizon.

Average Lease Term

A business statistic representing the average lease term observed for all leases falling within a LTC. It is used as the representative lease term for the LTC in optimization and post-optimization.

Remaining Constrained Arr Dmd

Constrained demand is the demand that will be accepted subject to supply limitations.

Lease Term Category Reference Month

Represents the most common lease term within the lease term category.

LEM End Month

The calendar month (i.e. January, February etc.) that a particular lease term ends for lease expiration management purposes. The number of recommended expirations vs total expirations for that month determines if a lease term should be priced lower (encouraged) or priced higher (discouraged).

LEM Actual Expirations

The number of actual leases that will be expiring within the corresponding end month for that lease term.

LEM Remaining Unconstrained Demand

The number of expirations expected from demand which is forecasted to materialize in the future.

LEM Estimated Expirations

The number of leases that LRO is recommending expire within that particular end month for the lease term based on the seasonality for that month, the number of move-ins vs move-outs, and expirations over the past 12 full calendar months.
